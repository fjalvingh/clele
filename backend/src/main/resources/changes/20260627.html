Barcode scanner

A new Barcode Scan screen (in the sidebar) lets you add stock rapidly using a USB barcode scanner or by typing a part number.

  • The input is always focused and ready — just scan the next barcode.
  • Parts are looked up in your local inventory first; if not found, an online search runs automatically.
  • For known parts, enter a location, quantity and optional price and hit Add Stock.
  • For new parts found online, Create & Add Stock creates the part and adds the first stock entry in one step.
  • After saving or skipping, the screen returns immediately to the scan input for the next part.
  • A Recent scans list at the bottom shows what you've processed during the current session.

Stock thresholds redesigned

Minimum stock levels are now tracked per root location rather than per individual shelf or sub-location. This means the threshold for a part in "Building A" covers all stock spread across rooms and cupboards inside it.

  • The old per-location minimums have been migrated automatically (highest value wins when multiple sub-locations had a threshold).
  • Set or edit thresholds from the Stock Thresholds card on any part's detail page.
  • The Low Stock page now shows the total on-hand quantity across the whole subtree compared to the threshold.
  • Thresholds can only be placed on root locations (those with no parent).

Stock movements between locations

Moving stock between two locations is now recorded as a single movement in the history ledger, with clear "Moved to …" / "Moved from …" annotations on each end.

Weighted-average cost tracking

Unit prices are now maintained as a weighted average across all purchases. Adding more stock at a different price blends into the existing average rather than overwriting it, giving a more accurate picture of what your stock actually cost.

Projects

You can now create Projects and attach a bill of materials (BOM) to each one.

  • Add parts to a project with a required quantity.
  • Reserve stock for the project directly from the project detail page.
  • See at a glance how much stock is allocated versus still needed per part.

Part number is now the primary identifier

Parts no longer have a separate "name" field. The part number and description are now used everywhere instead, matching the way real component catalogues work.

Part counts on the Categories page

Each category in the tree now shows a small badge with the number of parts directly assigned to it, making it easy to spot populated vs. empty categories at a glance.
